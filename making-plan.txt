TechTO Tangerine Making Plan
============================

Working product concept:
Tangerine Pathfinder, a friendly AI money coach for newcomer new grads starting
their first job in Canada. The product helps them understand what is happening
with their money, what to do next, and which actions matter most.

Demo promise:
"Angel just started her first Canadian job. Tangerine Pathfinder turns her
confusing payroll, bank fees, credit card options, and RRSP matching deadline
into a clear first-year financial roadmap."

MVP user:
- Angel Chen, 22
- Newcomer to Canada and recent Canadian university graduate
- Starting a new job
- Has a campus bank account that now charges fees
- Has one no-rewards credit card
- Feels embarrassed asking basic financial questions
- Distrusts bank tellers because she worries they are upselling her

Core user flow:
1. Landing / product intro
   - Explain that this is a guided AI money coach, not a generic chatbot.
   - Primary action: "Build my first financial roadmap."

2. Personalization quiz
   - Ask financial knowledge level.
   - Ask status: newcomer, new grad, first job, first home buyer, or other.
   - Ask top goals: emergency fund, reduce fees, improve credit, invest, save
     for a home, send money home, pay debt, file taxes.
   - Ask language preference and whether the user wants plain-language mode.

3. Optional linking step
   - Offer to link bank and payroll information.
   - Make this optional for trust.
   - Explain exactly what linking helps with and what the user can turn off.

4. Trust and data controls
   - Show settings for linked accounts, long-term memory, reminders, and data
     sharing.
   - Let Angel review and edit what the AI remembers.
   - Let Angel continue with sample data if she does not want to link accounts.

5. Personalized dashboard
   - Generate dashboard sections based on the quiz.
   - Show only what Angel needs now to reduce overwhelm.
   - Suggested sections:
     - Paycheque explained
     - Bank fees and account fit
     - Credit card upgrade path
     - RRSP matching task
     - First-year financial roadmap
     - Ask in plain language

6. Initial AI report
   - Explain after-tax pay and deductions.
   - Flag mystery bank fees and suggest a no-fee account path.
   - Explain why her current credit card may not fit her needs.
   - Identify urgent actions, especially RRSP matching.
   - Cite references or source labels for recommendations.

7. RRSP matching walkthrough
   - Detect or ask for the employer deadline.
   - Calculate the contribution percentage and dollar amount needed to capture
     the full employer match.
   - Explain RRSP contribution room in plain language.
   - Recommend a reasonable fund path or explain the tradeoffs.
   - Walk through the enrolment form field by field.
   - Be clear that Angel must submit the legal enrolment herself.

8. Roadmap visualization
   - Show 30, 60, and 90 day actions.
   - Show month 3 to 6 progress goals.
   - Include milestones such as:
     - Stop unnecessary bank fees
     - Capture employer RRSP match
     - Choose a better credit card path
     - Build emergency fund
     - Understand TFSA vs RRSP
     - Prepare for first tax filing

9. Guided AI support
   - Avoid making the main interface a blank chat box.
   - Use cards, buttons, and prompts such as:
     - "Explain this like I am new to Canada"
     - "What should I do first?"
     - "Show me the safest option"
     - "What could hurt my credit?"
     - "Translate this into simpler language"

Prototype screens to build:
- Landing screen
- Personalization quiz
- Optional linking and trust controls
- Dashboard
- Initial report
- RRSP matching walkthrough
- Roadmap visualization
- Settings / memory review

AI behavior to demonstrate:
- Classifies Angel's life stage from quiz answers.
- Uses plain language and reassurance.
- Produces a structured dashboard, not only chat text.
- Gives source-backed recommendations.
- Remembers goals and previous choices.
- Shows proactive nudges such as RRSP deadline reminders.
- Explains what it cannot do automatically and where user consent is required.

Design priorities:
- Warm, non-judgmental tone.
- Low cognitive load.
- Tangerine brand fit: simple, friendly, clear, optimistic.
- Multilingual and multicultural affordances.
- Trust at every step, especially around linked accounts and AI memory.
- Celebrate concrete wins instead of abstract financial literacy.

Build priorities:
1. Static prototype with believable sample data for Angel.
2. Quiz state that changes dashboard content.
3. Generated-looking initial report and roadmap.
4. RRSP matching guided task.
5. Trust controls screen.
6. Optional AI/chat panel if time allows.

Suggested implementation approach:
- Use a simple web app with hard-coded Angel sample data.
- Keep data local for the prototype.
- Use reusable components for cards, steps, recommendations, and roadmap items.
- If using an LLM during the demo, keep it scoped to explanation and Q&A.
- If LLM integration is too risky for the deadline, simulate AI output with
  deterministic content and focus the demo on the product experience.

Demo story:
1. Angel opens Tangerine Pathfinder after seeing her first confusing paycheque.
2. She completes a short quiz and chooses plain-language support.
3. She optionally links payroll and banking data, with clear trust controls.
4. The dashboard is generated around her actual needs.
5. The initial report explains her paycheque, bank fees, and first actions.
6. The AI flags RRSP matching as urgent and walks her through the process.
7. Angel sees a 30/60/90 day roadmap and feels clear about what to do next.

What to avoid:
- A generic finance chatbot with no structured outcomes.
- Too many target segments in the demo.
- Overpromising full automation for legal or banking actions.
- Product recommendations that feel like upselling.
- Dense financial education pages without a next step.
