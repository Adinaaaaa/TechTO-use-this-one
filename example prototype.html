i What is income tax?
Income tax is what you pay to the government based on how much you earn. Canada uses two layers: federal tax (goes to Ottawa) and provincial tax (stays in Ontario). The more you earn, the higher the percentage you pay — this is called a "progressive" tax system.

i What is CPP?
CPP stands for Canada Pension Plan. Think of it as a forced retirement savings account. Every pay period you contribute, and your employer matches it dollar-for-dollar. When you retire (or become disabled), you receive monthly payments from this fund.

i What is EI?
EI stands for Employment Insurance. It's like insurance for your job — if you lose your job or take parental leave, EI pays you a portion of your income while you look for work or care for your family. You pay a small premium every paycheque to be covered.

Every year, the CRA gives you a limit on how much you can put into your RRSP. It grows at 18% of last year's income. Think of it as a bucket — once it's full for the year, you can't add more.
