Research summary:
- The research is exploring how Tangerine could better support financially
  stressful life moments, especially for newcomers, digital-first users, new
  grads, and first-time home buyers.
- The target users appear to need help understanding Canadian financial systems,
  building credit, managing bills, budgeting day to day, and making large
  decisions such as renting, buying a home, or choosing financial products.
- Existing pain points include financial anxiety, confusing jargon, uncertainty
  about what to do next, difficulty comparing options, limited trust in generic
  financial advice, and a lack of culturally relevant or life-stage-specific
  guidance.
- Competitor notes compare tools such as Borrowell, Pill, and Marcus AI. The
  board highlights gaps around personalization, multilingual support, cultural
  finance context, coaching, and actionable step-by-step planning.
- Differentiation opportunities for Tangerine include life-stage
  personalization, multilingual and cultural finance support, conversational
  financial coaching, milestone-driven roadmaps, jargon-free financial literacy,
  and a clearer first-home-buyer journey.
- Early product ideas include a Tangerine budgeting or banking suite with tools
  such as a Cash Back Calculator, Budget Tool, and Money Rules Tool.
- Tangerine's relevant strengths for newcomers include digital-first banking,
  no-fee or low-friction account access, a broad ATM network, cashback-oriented
  products, and newcomer-focused mortgage support.
- Open questions include which target user segment should be prioritized first,
  what financial moment matters most, how much guidance should be automated, and
  how Tangerine can build trust without feeling generic or overwhelming.
