Research summary:
- The research is exploring how Tangerine could better support financially
  stressful life moments, with the sharpest MVP focus on newcomer new grads who
  are starting work in Canada and do not yet understand the Canadian financial
  system.
- The core persona is Angel Chen, 22. She is a new grad and newcomer to Canada
  who just started a new job. Her only bank account is a campus account, she now
  sees fees she does not understand, has one no-rewards credit card, and feels
  embarrassed and distrustful when trying to get help from banks.
- The target users need confidence and clarity more than more financial
  complexity. They need to understand Canadian banking, credit, payroll
  deductions, registered accounts, taxes, fees, and what to do first.
- Existing pain points include financial anxiety, confusing jargon, uncertainty
  about what to do next, difficulty comparing options, limited trust in generic
  financial advice, and a lack of culturally relevant or life-stage-specific
  guidance.
- Competitor notes compare tools such as Borrowell, Pill, and Marcus AI. The
  board highlights gaps around personalization, multilingual support, cultural
  finance context, coaching, and actionable step-by-step planning.
- Differentiation opportunities for Tangerine include identity-aware guidance,
  multilingual and cultural finance support, conversational coaching with memory,
  milestone-driven roadmaps, jargon-free financial literacy, trust controls, and
  action-oriented next steps rather than static reports.
- The strongest product wedge is a friendly AI money coach that answers: "What
  should I do next with my money?" For Angel, the first valuable moments are
  explaining her paycheque, identifying mystery bank fees, recommending a better
  account or credit card path, and guiding her through RRSP matching.
- Early product ideas include a personalization quiz, optional bank and payroll
  linking, a personalized dashboard, an initial report based on linked data, a
  roadmap visualization, financial education prompts, generated Q&A, reminders,
  safety settings, memory review, and personalized reports with references.
- Tangerine's relevant strengths for newcomers include digital-first banking,
  no-fee or low-friction account access, a broad ATM network, cashback-oriented
  products, and newcomer-focused mortgage support.
- Open questions include which target user segment should be prioritized first,
  what financial moment matters most, how much guidance should be automated, and
  how Tangerine can build trust without feeling generic or overwhelming.

Chosen MVP direction:
- Focus on Angel's first-year-in-Canada / first-job financial roadmap.
- Start with a short personalization quiz before optional data linking.
- Generate a personalized dashboard and tutorial so Angel does not need to know
  what to ask.
- Generate an initial report that explains payroll, spending, bank fees, and
  urgent financial actions in plain language.
- Include trust controls: review long-term memory, manage linked accounts,
  toggle data sharing, and explain recommendations with sources.
- Use the AI as a guided agent, not just a chat box. The interface should rely on
  dashboard sections, decision cards, reports, buttons, and walkthroughs, with
  chat available as support.
- For RRSP matching, the AI cannot fully automate legal employee enrolment, but
  it can detect deadlines, calculate the contribution needed for the full match,
  recommend a fund, explain contribution room, and guide the form field by field.

Hackathon guidance:
- Build for Track 2: Tangerine - AI Agents to Organize Your Financial Life.
- The solution should help people understand, organize, and optimize their
  financial life by reducing stress and improving financial confidence.
- Strong ideas should go beyond a simple chatbot. The agent should take action,
  remember context, coordinate tasks, make decisions, or enable workflows.
- Judging criteria emphasize meaningful real-world problems, creative and
  delightful experiences, and thoughtful use of AI agents.
- The demo must include a live demo in the three-minute submission video.
- All code should be created during the hackathon. Open-source libraries and
  third-party tools are allowed.
- Submission deadline is 5pm. Shortlisted teams should be ready for a live demo
  and Q&A.
